## Reflection

### 1. What went well during this unit?

Our group successfully established a clear vision for Mitra by identifying a specific gap in existing tools like NotebookLM. Additionally, we recognized the need for a tool that balances high-level information synthesis with the practical day-to-day functional needs for students. We were also able to collaborate effectively to define a solution and necessary features needed to improve the students workflow.

### 2. What were some challenges your group faced in this unit?

One of the primary challenges was narrowing down our concept to ensure the app remained focused on self-organized learning rather than becoming a generic task manager. We had to think critically about how we would combine the app’s organizational features with its tailored suggestions. Our goal was to ensure that AI didn't just pull information, but actively supported the user’s daily academic responsibilities and long-term goals.

### 3. What additional support will you need in upcoming units as you continue to work on your final project?

As we move into implementation, we may need support in integrating AI-powered suggestions to ensure they accurately produce relevant content. Additionally, we may need guidance on structuring the database to handle user history, tagged course materials, and chronological journal entries.
